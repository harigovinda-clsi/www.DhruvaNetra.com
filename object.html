Technical Notes

Object 2024 MX1 was discovered on March 18, 2024 by the Catalina Sky Survey. It belongs to the Apollo group of near-Earth asteroids, characterized by orbits that cross Earth's orbital path. The object's semi-major axis of 1.42 AU and moderate eccentricity place it in a resonant relationship with Earth, leading to periodic close approaches roughly every 3-4 years.

Spectroscopic observations suggest an S-type composition, indicating a stony asteroid likely composed of silicate minerals and metals. The rotation period of approximately 4.7 hours is within the typical range for objects of this size. Physical characterization efforts continue with each apparition.

While classified as potentially hazardous due to its size and orbital characteristics, current trajectory calculations show no impact risk for at least the next century. The object serves as an important test case for planetary defense observation capabilities and orbital prediction methodologies.
